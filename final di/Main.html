Every city has morals and rights;

Every head has its own mind;

Every heart has its own love,

Each throat has its own taste,

And I have only one thought in the world,

And I just can't lose my mind.

Peter, for the ranks of lords, rubs corners,

Fedka, a merchant, always lies at Arshina.

He builds his house in a new way,

It's all in percentages, believe me!

And I have only one thought in the world,

And I just can't lose my mind.

He constantly pulls the soil,

This foreigner raises cattle.

That is formed for catching dogs,

The house is noisy with guests like a tavern -

And I have only one thought in the world,

And I just can't lose my mind.

Builds a law lawyer to his own tone,

The student's head is pounding from the dispute.

They are disturbed by Venus Cupid,

Everyone's head is tormented by his own stupidity,

And I have only one thought in the world,

It's not crazy for me to die.

Death is terrible, swinging obliquely!

You don't even spare royal hair,

You don't look where the man is and where the king is,

You eat everything like straw in a fire.

Who spits sharp steel at her?

The one whose conscience is like clear crystal..
